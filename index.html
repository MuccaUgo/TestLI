Leadership — expanded concepts

Each card has meaning, recognition signals and the typical exam trap.

Foundations
Leadership vs Management

Meaning. Management coordinates people and resources to achieve objectives efficiently: planning, organizing, directing and controlling. Leadership creates direction, mobilizes people, shapes meaning and sustains commitment under uncertainty. The course does not say that one is good and the other bad: it says they answer different problems.

How to recognize it in a case

Management signals: procedures, roles, resources, targets, control, efficiency. Leadership signals: vision, influence, meaning, engagement, change, shared direction.

Typical trap

A formal manager is not automatically showing leadership. If the case only shows control and execution, do not overread it as leadership.

Foundations
Leader vs Leadership

Meaning. A leader is an individual who influences others. Leadership is broader: a dynamic interaction among leaders, followers and context. The course moves from the heroic leader to leading as a process that can be vertical, shared, emergent and contextual.

How to recognize it in a case

Heroic individual = leader-centric. Reciprocal influence, rotating roles, group process = leadership-as-process.

Typical trap

No single boss does not mean no leadership. It may be shared leadership.

Foundations
Leadership and Sensemaking

Meaning. In innovation, leadership means helping people interpret ambiguous and uncertain situations. Sensemaking creates a shared understanding of what is happening, why it matters and what direction is worth pursuing.

How to recognize it in a case

Ambiguous challenge, unclear future, people interpreting weak signals, shared narrative, reframing.

Typical trap

Sensemaking is not just communication. It is meaning construction under uncertainty.

Foundations
Trait Theory

Meaning. Trait theory explains leadership through stable personal attributes such as charisma, intelligence, self-confidence, energy or integrity. It is useful historically but limited because leadership cannot be reduced to personality.

How to recognize it in a case

The case explains effectiveness mainly through personal qualities.

Typical trap

Most exam cases describe behaviors, follower readiness, rewards or team processes. Use those models instead when they fit.

Behavioral theories
Behavioral Theory

Meaning. Behavioral approaches ask what leaders do. They focus on observable actions, especially task/production orientation and people/relationship orientation.

How to recognize it in a case

Setting goals, monitoring, listening, supporting, involving, controlling, developing.

Typical trap

Do not infer a style from personality adjectives. Use behavior evidence.

Behavioral theories
Managerial Grid — Blake & Mouton

Meaning. The grid maps leadership on two axes: concern for production and concern for people. The main styles are Team Management, Authority-Compliance, Country Club, Middle-of-the-Road and Impoverished.

How to recognize it in a case

Ask two questions: is the leader pushing results, standards and deadlines? Is the leader caring about morale, involvement and people needs?

Typical trap

Being nice is not Team Management. Team Management requires high people concern and high production concern.

Behavioral theories
Team Management

Meaning. High concern for people and high concern for production. The leader builds trust, collaboration and commitment while also keeping standards and goals high.

How to recognize it in a case

Ambitious goals, progress tracking, accountability, plus support, listening, recognition and team cohesion.

Typical trap

If there is care but weak accountability, it is not Team Management; it is closer to Country Club or comfort zone.

Behavioral theories
Authority-Compliance Management

Meaning. High concern for production and low concern for people. The leader emphasizes discipline, output, control, precision and deadlines, often at the cost of morale.

How to recognize it in a case

Strict schedules, close monitoring, “results are all that matters”, dismissal of participation.

Typical trap

Short-term productivity does not change the classification: people concern remains low.

Behavioral theories
Country Club Management

Meaning. High concern for people and low concern for production. The leader protects harmony and comfort but does not push standards enough.

How to recognize it in a case

Friendly climate, emotional support, conflict avoidance, but deadlines or quality slip.

Typical trap

Country Club is not psychological safety. Psychological safety must be paired with accountability.

Behavioral theories
Middle-of-the-Road Management

Meaning. Moderate concern for people and production. It seeks acceptable compromise but usually lacks strong commitment to either excellence or deep human engagement.

How to recognize it in a case

Compromise, moderate pressure, moderate support, avoidance of extremes.

Typical trap

Balanced does not mean excellent. Team Management is high-high, not medium-medium.

Behavioral theories
Impoverished Management

Meaning. Low concern for people and low concern for production. The leader does the minimum and avoids real involvement.

How to recognize it in a case

No guidance, no support, no follow-up, low accountability.

Typical trap

Do not confuse it with Delegating. Delegating is intentional and works only with ready followers.

Situational leadership
Situational Leadership

Meaning. No leadership style is always best. The leader adapts task direction and relational support to follower readiness, meaning competence and commitment/confidence.

How to recognize it in a case

Different team members have different experience, motivation or confidence; the best style changes by person.

Typical trap

The classic trap is using the same autonomy for everyone. Empowerment without readiness becomes confusion.

Situational leadership
Telling / Directing

Meaning. High task direction, low support. The leader explains what to do, how to do it and monitors closely.

How to recognize it in a case

New hire, low competence, unclear procedures, needs explicit instruction.

Typical trap

Telling is not automatically bad. It is appropriate when readiness is low.

Situational leadership
Selling / Coaching

Meaning. High direction and high support. The leader still gives structure but also explains, encourages and builds confidence.

How to recognize it in a case

Motivated or willing person who lacks task or context competence.

Typical trap

If the person is competent but hesitant, too much direction may be wrong; Participating may fit better.

Situational leadership
Participating / Supporting

Meaning. Low direction and high support. The follower can do the work but lacks confidence, motivation or commitment.

How to recognize it in a case

Experienced or technically capable person who hesitates, disengages or needs involvement.

Typical trap

Do not use Participating for someone who does not know how to do the task.

Situational leadership
Delegating

Meaning. Low direction and low support. The leader gives autonomy because the follower is competent, confident and committed.

How to recognize it in a case

Senior expert, proactive, self-driven, able to own the task.

Typical trap

Delegating too early becomes abandonment. Delegating to the right person is not laissez-faire.

Transactional leadership
Transactional Leadership

Meaning. Leadership as exchange: clear goals, measurable performance, rewards for achievement and corrective actions for deviations. It creates predictability and accountability.

How to recognize it in a case

KPIs, dashboards, quotas, bonuses, sanctions, reviews, corrective workshops.

Typical trap

Autonomy in how people work does not exclude transactional leadership if goals, evaluation and rewards are centrally controlled.

Transactional leadership
Contingent Reward

Meaning. A reward is explicitly tied to performance: if a target is met, people receive money, recognition, privileges or opportunities.

How to recognize it in a case

“If you reach X, you get Y.”

Typical trap

If the question asks about monitoring deviations, Active/Passive Management by Exception may be more precise.

Transactional leadership
Active Management by Exception

Meaning. The leader actively monitors performance and intervenes early to prevent or correct deviations from standards.

How to recognize it in a case

Dashboards, weekly checks, early warnings, preventive correction.

Typical trap

If the leader waits until failure is visible, it is passive, not active.

Transactional leadership
Passive Management by Exception

Meaning. The leader intervenes only after standards have been violated or performance has clearly dropped.

How to recognize it in a case

Corrective action after the problem appears; limited intervention before failure.

Typical trap

Passive is not the same as laissez-faire. Passive still reacts to deviations.

Transactional leadership
Laissez-faire

Meaning. Avoidance or absence of leadership. The leader gives little guidance, little support, little monitoring and avoids decisions.

How to recognize it in a case

Disengagement, absence, no follow-up, avoidance of responsibility.

Typical trap

Do not call low-control delegation laissez-faire when the follower is ready and the autonomy is intentional.

Transformational leadership
Transformational Leadership

Meaning. Transformational leadership changes how people understand themselves, their work and the future. It goes beyond rewards and compliance through vision, purpose, creativity, development and role modelling.

How to recognize it in a case

Vision, culture change, challenging assumptions, personal growth, values, purpose.

Typical trap

A charismatic leader can still be mainly transactional if the mechanism is targets and rewards.

Transformational leadership
Inspirational Motivation

Meaning. The leader articulates an attractive future and connects daily work to a larger purpose.

How to recognize it in a case

Clear vision, energizing language, purpose, future direction.

Typical trap

A motivational speech alone is not enough to prove full transformational leadership.

Transformational leadership
Intellectual Stimulation

Meaning. The leader encourages people to question assumptions, think differently and generate unconventional solutions.

How to recognize it in a case

Brainstorming, hackathons, challenging old routines, bold ideas.

Typical trap

Asking people to work harder is not intellectual stimulation. It must change thinking.

Transformational leadership
Individualized Consideration

Meaning. The leader pays attention to each person’s development, needs and aspirations through coaching or tailored support.

How to recognize it in a case

Mentoring, listening to aspirations, personal development, tailored guidance.

Typical trap

One-on-one meetings focused only on metrics and gaps are not necessarily individualized consideration.

Transformational leadership
Idealized Influence

Meaning. The leader becomes a role model because they embody values, integrity and commitment that followers trust and admire.

How to recognize it in a case

Consistent values, moral example, credibility, admired behavior.

Typical trap

Showing exact procedures is not automatically idealized influence; it may be standardization.

Distributed leadership
Shared Leadership

Meaning. Leadership influence is distributed across team members. Different people lead at different moments depending on expertise, task and context.

How to recognize it in a case

Rotating responsibilities, collective decisions, peer influence, participatory workshops, expertise-based leadership.

Typical trap

Shared leadership is not no leadership. It is active horizontal coordination.

Distributed leadership
Servant Leadership

Meaning. The leader is servant first: the focus is helping others grow healthier, wiser, freer, more autonomous and more able to serve others.

How to recognize it in a case

“How can I help?”, removing obstacles, developing people, humility, empowerment, care for the least privileged.

Typical trap

Servant leadership is not simple kindness. It is a leadership logic based on serving growth.

Psychological safety
Psychological Safety

Meaning. A shared belief that people can speak up, ask questions, admit errors and challenge ideas without fear of humiliation or punishment. Essential in uncertain, interdependent innovation work.

How to recognize it in a case

The leader frames work as learning, admits fallibility and invites voice.

Typical trap

Psychological safety is not comfort or avoidance of tension. It enables difficult conversations.

Psychological safety
Safety + Accountability Matrix

Meaning. Safety without accountability creates comfort zone. Accountability without safety creates anxiety zone. Low both creates apathy. High both creates learning zone.

How to recognize it in a case

Learning zone = voice plus serious standards. Comfort zone = friendly but weak results. Anxiety zone = pressure and fear.

Typical trap

The best innovation climate is not “everyone feels good”; it is high safety plus high accountability.

Team dynamics
Team vs Group

Meaning. A group can be individuals working independently. A team has complementary skills, collective goals, coordinated work, common identity and mutual accountability.

How to recognize it in a case

Coordinated work, collective evaluation, shared goals, complementary roles.

Typical trap

People in the same project are not automatically a team.

Team dynamics
Tuckman Model

Meaning. Teams develop through Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing and Adjourning. Conflict can be a development phase, not necessarily failure.

How to recognize it in a case

Forming = orientation; Storming = tension; Norming = shared rules; Performing = effective collaboration; Adjourning = closure.

Typical trap

Avoiding storming can block real team development.

Motivation
Intrinsic vs Extrinsic Motivation

Meaning. Intrinsic motivation comes from the activity itself: curiosity, learning, autonomy, mastery, meaning. Extrinsic motivation comes from external outcomes: money, recognition, status, avoiding punishment.

How to recognize it in a case

Enjoyment and learning = intrinsic. Bonus, praise or avoiding reprimands = extrinsic.

Typical trap

Many people show both. Pick the dominant driver in the case.

Motivation
Maslow

Meaning. People are motivated by needs: physiological, safety, belonging, esteem and self-actualization.

How to recognize it in a case

Identify which need is activated or threatened.

Typical trap

Money is not always the relevant need; the case may be about belonging, esteem or growth.

Motivation
Herzberg

Meaning. Hygiene factors prevent dissatisfaction; motivators create deeper satisfaction. Hygiene includes pay and conditions; motivators include achievement, recognition, responsibility and growth.

How to recognize it in a case

Better conditions stop complaints; responsibility and achievement create engagement.

Typical trap

Improving hygiene does not automatically create motivation.

Motivation
McClelland

Meaning. People differ in dominant needs: achievement, affiliation and power/influence.

How to recognize it in a case

Achievement = challenge/results. Affiliation = belonging/relationships. Power = influence/impact.

Typical trap

Power is not always negative; it can be constructive influence.

Motivation
Vroom Expectancy Theory

Meaning. Motivation depends on three links: effort leads to performance, performance leads to outcomes, outcomes are valued.

How to recognize it in a case

Will my effort matter? Will performance be rewarded? Do I value the reward?

Typical trap

A reward fails if people do not believe they can reach it or do not value it.

Motivation
Equity Theory

Meaning. People compare their input-output ratio with others. Perceived unfairness damages motivation even when absolute rewards look acceptable.

How to recognize it in a case

Comparisons, resentment, unequal treatment.

Typical trap

Motivation is about perceived justice, not just objective compensation.

Motivation
Self-Determination Theory

Meaning. High-quality motivation grows with autonomy, competence and relatedness. Very relevant for innovation because creativity needs room, mastery and connection.

How to recognize it in a case

Choice, mastery, capability, belonging.

Typical trap

Autonomy without competence or support can create anxiety.

Decision making
Organizational Decision Making

Meaning. Innovation decisions are rarely fully rational: problems are ambiguous, data may be incomplete, criteria are contested and the future is emerging.

How to recognize it in a case

Unclear goals, incomplete data, political negotiation, learning by doing.

Typical trap

Do not apply full rationality to ambiguous innovation problems.

Decision making
Bounded Rationality

Meaning. Real decision-makers have incomplete information, limited cognitive capacity and limited time. They satisfice rather than maximize.

How to recognize it in a case

Good-enough choices, limited analysis, time pressure.

Typical trap

Bounded rationality is descriptive: how people actually decide.

Decision making
Rational / Process / Political / Anarchy Models

Meaning. Decision models depend on problem certainty and solution knowledge. Rational works when both are clear; process/incremental when solution is uncertain; political when consensus is uncertain; anarchy/garbage can when both are highly uncertain.

How to recognize it in a case

Certain problem + certain solution = rational. Uncertain solution = trial and error. Uncertain consensus = politics. High uncertainty = garbage can.

Typical trap

Do not claim rational decision-making when goals and solutions are contested.

Decision making
Garbage Can Model

Meaning. In organized anarchies, problems, solutions, participants and choices are loosely coupled. Decisions happen when these streams collide.

How to recognize it in a case

Fluid preferences, unclear technology, fluid participation, solutions looking for problems.

Typical trap

In garbage can models organizations do not simply solve problems; problems, people and solutions collide.

Decision making
Critical Thinking for the Report

Meaning. Critical thinking moves from description to theoretical interpretation and problematization: what happened, why it happened through theory, and what new question emerges.

How to recognize it in a case

Events + theoretical lens + emerging question.

Typical trap

A chronology is not critical thinking. You need interpretation.

Leadership common traps
Trap
Shared leadership ≠ absence of leadership

There may be no single formal leader, but leadership is still active through reciprocal influence and rotating responsibility.

Trap
Delegating ≠ laissez-faire

Delegating works with competent and committed followers. Laissez-faire is avoidance.

Trap
Selling ≠ Participating

Selling = high direction + high support. Participating = low direction + high support.

Trap
Psychological safety ≠ comfort

The learning zone requires safety and accountability together.

Trap
One-on-one meeting ≠ individualized consideration

If the meeting only reviews metrics and gaps, it is transactional.

Trap
Transformational ≠ charismatic speech

Look for vision, intellectual stimulation, individual development and role modelling.

Trap
Team Management ≠ Country Club

Team Management is high people and high production. Country Club is high people but low production.

Trap
Passive management by exception ≠ laissez-faire

Passive still reacts after deviations; laissez-faire avoids leadership responsibility.

Innovation — expanded concepts

Each card has meaning, recognition signals and the typical exam trap.

Foundations
Innovation as Sensemaking

Meaning. Innovation is not only producing a new product. It is interpreting uncertainty, deciding what matters and creating a new direction that people can understand and support.

How to recognize it in a case

Teams frame ambiguous challenges, negotiate meaning and turn weak signals into a concept.

Typical trap

Do not separate innovation content from leadership process. In this course, both are linked.

Foundations
Innovation of Solution vs Innovation of Meaning

Meaning. Innovation of Solution changes HOW and WHAT: features, functions, channels, processes, touchpoints and experience. Innovation of Meaning changes WHY: the symbolic, cultural or emotional reason people value something.

How to recognize it in a case

HOW = functional; WHAT = multisensory/customer experience; WHY = symbolic.

Typical trap

A better feature is not a new meaning. A nice story is not enough unless the WHY changes.

Approaches
Market Pull Innovation

Meaning. Market Pull starts from user needs, market signals, social trends and observed behaviors. It is close to human-centered design: understand people, prototype, test and adapt.

How to recognize it in a case

Explicit demand, pain points, user feedback, market pressure, consumer behavior.

Typical trap

If a firm starts from internal R&D and later finds a market, it is Technology Push.

Approaches
Human-Centered Design

Meaning. A creative problem-solving process that starts with people and ends with solutions tailored to their needs. It uses empathy, observation, ideation, prototyping and feedback.

How to recognize it in a case

Immersion in users’ lives, inspiration, ideation, prototypes, testing.

Typical trap

Asking users directly is not always enough; the course stresses understanding what people value in context.

Approaches
Technology Push Innovation

Meaning. Technology Push starts from scientific or technological competence. A firm develops or owns a technology and then searches for applications.

How to recognize it in a case

R&D program, patent, lab discovery, scientific breakthrough, internal development, no initial market demand.

Typical trap

Technology inside the product does not prove Technology Push. The driver must be technology.

Approaches
Design Push / Design-Driven Innovation

Meaning. Design Push starts from language, culture and meaning. It proposes a new interpretation of why a product or service matters.

How to recognize it in a case

New symbolic purpose, cultural reframing, inside-out vision, interpreters.

Typical trap

If the case mainly follows existing consumer preferences, it is more likely Market Pull.

Technology
Technological Paradigm and Trajectory

Meaning. A technological paradigm is a model for solving selected technical problems; a trajectory is the pattern of normal improvement within that paradigm.

How to recognize it in a case

Continuous technical improvement along a known path.

Typical trap

A trajectory is technical, not just a market trend.

Technology
S-Curves

Meaning. Technologies often improve slowly, then rapidly, then slow as limits appear. A new technology may initially underperform and later overtake the old one.

How to recognize it in a case

Performance over time, early experimentation, acceleration, maturity, saturation.

Typical trap

The current best technology is not always the future winner.

Technology
Technological Discontinuity

Meaning. A break that opens a new technological trajectory and changes the basis of competition.

How to recognize it in a case

New principles, new trajectory, old capabilities less useful.

Typical trap

A strong improvement within the same path is not a discontinuity.

Technology
Dominant Design

Meaning. A product architecture becomes the accepted industry standard. Competitors converge around it and infrastructure adapts.

How to recognize it in a case

Widespread adoption, competitors copying, standardization, complementary infrastructure.

Typical trap

Dominant design is not necessarily the first or most radical invention.

Technology
Incremental Innovation

Meaning. A limited improvement to an existing product, process or service without changing architecture, meaning or market logic.

How to recognize it in a case

Better performance, small feature, efficiency gain.

Typical trap

Do not call it incremental if the WHY or platform logic changes.

Technology
Radical Innovation

Meaning. A major break from existing solutions, often involving new technology, architecture or practices.

How to recognize it in a case

Substantial novelty, major change, technological discontinuity.

Typical trap

Radical is not the same as disruptive.

Technology
Architectural Innovation

Meaning. Existing components are recombined into a new system architecture. Components may be known; relationships change.

How to recognize it in a case

Reconfiguration of known elements.

Typical trap

If the essence is symbolic reinterpretation, it is Innovation of Meaning.

Technology
Competence Enhancing vs Destroying

Meaning. Competence-enhancing builds on incumbents’ knowledge. Competence-destroying makes old capabilities less valuable and requires new ones.

How to recognize it in a case

Ask whether incumbents’ existing know-how is strengthened or undermined.

Typical trap

Novelty alone is not enough; look at impact on capabilities.

Technology
Technology Epiphany

Meaning. An existing technology is reinterpreted or repurposed into a new domain, revealing unexpected value or meaning.

How to recognize it in a case

Existing technology, surprising new application, new field, new value.

Typical trap

If technology is newly developed and pushed to market, choose Technology Push.

Strategy
Resource-Based Perspective

Meaning. Innovation advantage depends on resources and capabilities: know-how, patents, routines, relationships, assets and complementary capabilities.

How to recognize it in a case

Firm-specific resources, difficult imitation, complementary assets.

Typical trap

Owning technology is not enough if the firm cannot exploit it.

Strategy
Disruptive Innovation

Meaning. Disruption usually starts with low-end customers or non-consumers through a simpler, cheaper or initially inferior solution, then improves and attacks the mainstream.

How to recognize it in a case

Low-end entry, overlooked users, non-consumption, initially inferior mainstream performance.

Typical trap

Not every successful or digital innovation is disruptive.

Meaning
Innovation of Meaning

Meaning. Innovation of Meaning redefines the cultural, emotional or symbolic purpose of a product/service. It changes why people engage with it.

How to recognize it in a case

From utility to identity; from control to care; from transaction to relationship; from object to story.

Typical trap

Sustainability, aesthetics or storytelling are not automatically meaning innovation unless WHY changes.

Meaning
Inside-Out Principle

Meaning. Inside-out innovation starts from an internal vision or interpretation of what could become meaningful, not simply from expressed demand.

How to recognize it in a case

Founder/team vision, personal interpretation, new purpose, new cultural direction.

Typical trap

Inside-out is not random creativity. It requires criticism, sparring and signals of change.

Meaning
Criticism vs Ideation

Meaning. Ideation generates possibilities; criticism questions assumptions and deepens meaning. Radical meanings do not come from brainstorming alone.

How to recognize it in a case

Challenging frames, questioning WHY, sparring, reframing before solutioning.

Typical trap

Many ideas are not the same as strong meaning innovation.

Meaning
Envisioning

Meaning. Envisioning moves from existing value drivers to new value drivers by exploring current and future HOW, WHAT and WHY.

How to recognize it in a case

Existing meaning/solution to new meaning/solution; “I want a solution because I love…”

Typical trap

A feature list is not envisioning. It must build a future interpretation of value.

Meaning
Sparring Partner

Meaning. A sparring partner challenges your vision, assumptions and meaning direction. The aim is stronger thinking, not comfort.

How to recognize it in a case

Active listening, critical reflection, questioning assumptions.

Typical trap

Sparring is not validation. If they only agree, it is weak sparring.

Meaning
Radical Circle

Meaning. The Radical Circle turns individual and pair envisioning into a shared innovation strategy and concept through criticism, negotiation and embodiment.

How to recognize it in a case

Circle discussion, multiple interpretations, manifesto, metaphor, scenario of meaning.

Typical trap

It is not ordinary group work; it is structured meaning construction.

Meaning
Scenario and Manifesto of Meaning

Meaning. The scenario compares movements from existing to new meanings; the manifesto makes the new direction explicit through reframed brief, signal of change, title and from-to transformation.

How to recognize it in a case

From-to transformation, metaphors, new WHY, signal of change.

Typical trap

A slogan is not a manifesto if it does not clarify the change in meaning.

Meaning
Winning from Within

Meaning. Before leading others, the innovator negotiates with themselves: values, tensions, assumptions, cognitive/emotional stance. This supports meaningful leadership under uncertainty.

How to recognize it in a case

Individual reflection, self-criticism, internal negotiation, emotional intelligence.

Typical trap

It is not decoration; it shapes the ability to lead innovation of meaning.

Meaning
Four Schools of Innovation Strategy

Meaning. The Four Schools help map innovation postures: outside-in or inside-out; planning, learning, competence/design or vision/meaning. Use them to explain the strategic logic behind a case.

How to recognize it in a case

Ask whether the process is market-driven, technology/learning-driven, competence/design-driven or visionary/meaning-driven.

Typical trap

Do not memorize labels mechanically. Use the driver of innovation.

GenAI
Co-Piloting, Co-Thinking, Co-Meaning

Meaning. AI can support execution, reflection or meaning-making. Co-Piloting improves productivity; Co-Thinking challenges assumptions; Co-Meaning helps reinterpret identity, purpose and value propositions.

How to recognize it in a case

Productivity = co-piloting. Critique/scenarios = co-thinking. Purpose/reframing = co-meaning.

Typical trap

The deeper AI enters meaning, the more you must guard human authorship and ethics.

Service Innovation
Service Innovation

Meaning. Service innovation designs a value co-creation experience across people, processes, technologies and touchpoints. The service is produced through interaction.

How to recognize it in a case

Customer journey, front-end/back-end, roles, processes, human and digital touchpoints.

Typical trap

Service innovation is not simply adding an app.

Service Innovation
Customer Journey Map

Meaning. The journey maps the experience before, during and after the service, identifying moments of truth, moments of meaning and redesign opportunities.

How to recognize it in a case

Awareness, core, reinforce; sequence of touchpoints; emotional and functional moments.

Typical trap

A journey map is not a random list of steps. It must show where value is created or lost.

Service Innovation
Moment of Truth vs Moment of Meaning

Meaning. A Moment of Truth is a critical service interaction where performance is judged. A Moment of Meaning is where the new symbolic WHY becomes visible and felt.

How to recognize it in a case

Truth = service quality/reliability. Meaning = symbolic transformation.

Typical trap

Not every touchpoint is a Moment of Meaning.

Service Innovation
Servuction Model

Meaning. The Servuction Model connects back-end and front-end: organization, processes, HRM, roles, technology, systems, logistics, human touchpoints and digital/physical touchpoints.

How to recognize it in a case

Visibility line, interaction line, people, processes, front-end/back-end.

Typical trap

A concept is weak if it describes only the customer side and ignores how the organization produces it.

Service Innovation
Service-Opportunity Matrix

Meaning. Service growth can happen through temporal expansion, spatial expansion, temporal reconfiguration or spatial reconfiguration, by adding or reconfiguring activities in primary or adjacent chains.

How to recognize it in a case

More times, more places, new sequencing, adjacent services.

Typical trap

Some service changes are solution innovations, not meaning innovations.

Platforms
Product Platform

Meaning. A product platform is a set of common components and linkages used to create a family of derivative products. It is not a product itself.

How to recognize it in a case

Modular base, shared architecture, derivatives, internal efficiency.

Typical trap

Product platform is not a two-sided market.

Platforms
Innovation Platform

Meaning. A technology/service acts as a foundation that external complementors can build upon, generating an ecosystem of complementary innovations.

How to recognize it in a case

Developers, complements, APIs, app stores, ecosystem.

Typical trap

If no external actors build complements, it is probably not an innovation platform.

Platforms
Transactional Platform

Meaning. A platform enables direct exchange between demand and supply sides and internalizes cross-side network effects.

How to recognize it in a case

Uber/Airbnb logic: two sides, transaction line, matchmaking.

Typical trap

The supply side is still a customer of the platform provider.

Platforms
Two-Sided Market Conditions

Meaning. A two-sided market needs distinct customer groups, indirect network effects and an intermediary able to internalize externalities.

How to recognize it in a case

Hosts need guests; guests need hosts. Developers need users; users need apps.

Typical trap

Two groups alone are not enough. There must be cross-side effects.

Platforms
Chicken-and-Egg Paradox

Meaning. Platforms struggle at launch because each side wants the other side to be present first. The provider must seed or subsidize one side to reach critical mass.

How to recognize it in a case

No suppliers without customers; no customers without suppliers.

Typical trap

This is a platform problem, not every startup problem.

Platforms
Orthogonal Platform — Client as Target

Meaning. One side uses the service while another side pays to reach that side’s attention. Advertising-based models are typical.

How to recognize it in a case

Users/viewers/readers as target; advertisers pay for attention.

Typical trap

If the third party buys data generated by users, it is client-as-source.

Platforms
Orthogonal Platform — Client as Source

Meaning. Users generate valuable data or traces; another side pays for aggregated insights or analytics.

How to recognize it in a case

Mobility data sold to municipalities; activity data sold as insights.

Typical trap

If the main value is a material transaction between two sides, it may be transactional.

Platforms
Hybrid / Multi-Sided Platform

Meaning. Platforms can combine transactional, orthogonal and innovation mechanisms. Successful platforms often evolve by adding sides and exploiting critical mass.

How to recognize it in a case

Multiple customer groups, different value flows, transactions plus ads/data/complements.

Typical trap

Do not force one type when multiple mechanisms coexist.

Platforms
Idle Asset Hunters

Meaning. Platform thinking helps firms identify idle assets — data, know-how, relationships, physical assets or user base — and use them to unlock new value.

How to recognize it in a case

Established firm uses existing assets to create a platform hub or add sides.

Typical trap

Platform thinking is not just launching a marketplace; it is exploiting underused assets through platform mechanisms.

Platforms
Platform Thinking

Meaning. Platform Thinking is the ability to use platform-based mechanisms to unlock digital business transformations. “Platform” may mean product, innovation, transactional or orthogonal platform.

How to recognize it in a case

Multiple mutually dependent customers, cross-side externalities, provider, sides and value flows.

Typical trap

“Platform” is a buzzword. Always ask what value creation mechanism is actually present.

Decision making
Organizational Decision Making in Innovation

Meaning. Innovation decisions differ from generic decisions: problems are ambiguous, data may not exist, criteria include desirability/plausibility/learning and leadership means building meaning.

How to recognize it in a case

Emerging future, contested legitimacy, incomplete data, learning opportunity.

Typical trap

Efficiency/ROI alone is not enough for early innovation projects.

Decision making
Normalization of Deviance

Meaning. Repeated acceptance of anomalies can make risk appear normal. The Challenger case shows how warning signals can be absorbed into routine because previous flights survived.

How to recognize it in a case

“We saw this risk before and nothing bad happened.”

Typical trap

Past survival does not prove safety; it may hide accumulated risk.

Innovation common traps
Trap
Market Pull ≠ Innovation of Meaning

Market Pull follows needs. Innovation of Meaning changes WHY people value something.

Trap
Technology Push ≠ Technology Epiphany

Technology Push starts from new/internal technology. Epiphany repurposes existing technology into a new domain.

Trap
Radical ≠ Disruptive

Disruption requires a low-end or new-market entry pattern, not just novelty.

Trap
Dominant Design ≠ First invention

The dominant design is the architecture that becomes the standard, not necessarily the first.

Trap
Platform ≠ digital app

A real platform has sides, network effects, complementors or orthogonal value flows.

Trap
Product Platform ≠ Transactional Platform

Product platform is internal modularity; transactional platform mediates exchanges.

Trap
Service Innovation ≠ App launch

Service innovation involves journey, touchpoints, front-end/back-end and value co-creation.

Trap
Creative marketing ≠ Innovation of Meaning

The communication must embody a new WHY, not just a nicer message.
